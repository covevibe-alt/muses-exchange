Acceptable use.

The rules for using Muse while it's a prototype.

● Last updated April 16, 2026
Plain-English summary Muse is a paper-trading prototype. It's free, it uses virtual money, it has no customer-support SLA. Be 18+, don't abuse the demo, don't scrape it, and don't treat anything here as financial advice. We can revoke access if any of that breaks down.

What Muse is

Muse is a paper-trading prototype that lets you trade a curated roster of music artists with virtual credits. Prices are derived from public streaming data (Spotify, YouTube). No real money is accepted, held, or paid out. Muse is not a regulated investment service and does not offer securities, derivatives, or any other financial instrument.

Who can use it

You should be at least 18 years old. The waitlist and the prototype are free to use. Your virtual balance, trades, and performance are stored locally in your browser and may be reset without notice as we iterate on the product.

What we ask of you

  • Don't use the prototype in ways that are obviously abusive — automated scraping, brute-force load testing, attempts to extract the artist list for commercial redistribution, that kind of thing.
  • Don't misrepresent the prototype as a real, licensed investment product. It isn't.
  • Don't harass artists listed on the platform. The roster is there because they're culturally interesting, not to invite abuse.

What we owe you

Honestly? Not much. The prototype is provided as-is, without warranties. We'll try to keep it up, but price fetches can fail, data can be delayed, and the whole thing can go offline if a dependency breaks. Don't build anything you care about on top of it.

Changes

As the project evolves — especially if Muse ever moves from prototype to a real, regulated product — we'll update these terms with notice on the site. Until then: be cool, have fun, tell us what breaks.
